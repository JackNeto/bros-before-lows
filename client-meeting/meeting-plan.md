# Bros Before Lows - Client Discovery Meeting Plan

**Meeting Duration:** 90 minutes
**Attendee:** Mathieu Harbec (Founder)
**Purpose:** Review and validate research findings, gather founder story, align on redesign priorities

---

## Pre-Meeting Checklist

- [ ] Send research summary document in advance (optional - or reveal in meeting)
- [ ] Confirm meeting time and location (video/in-person)
- [ ] Prepare screen share for presentation
- [ ] Have recording permission ready (for founder story capture)
- [ ] Print worksheets if in-person

---

## Agenda

### 1. Welcome & Context Setting (5 min)

**Talking Points:**
- Thank Mathieu for his time and the trust in this project
- Explain meeting goals: validate research, gather his story, prioritize features
- Set expectation: honest feedback is valuable - nothing is set in stone

**Script:**
> "Today I want to walk you through everything we've learned about BBL, the competitive landscape, and our recommendations for the redesign. Your job is to tell me what's right, what's wrong, and what's missing. The more honest you are, the better the final product will be."

---

### 2. Current State Review (10 min)

**Present:**
- Screenshot of current website
- Brand analysis summary (mission, messaging, branding)
- Current metrics: $5,950 raised, 105 donors, 6 cities

**Questions to Validate:**
1. Is the mission statement accurate? ("A movement for men's mental health grounded in healing, brotherhood and purpose")
2. Are there any key offerings or initiatives we missed?
3. What's working on the current site? What's frustrating?

---

### 3. Competitive Landscape (10 min)

**Present:**
- Overview of 5 competitors analyzed (Movember, HeadsUpGuys, ManUp, ManKind Project, Andy's Man Club)
- BBL's unique positioning: GTA focus + music therapy + peer support
- Differentiation opportunities

**Questions:**
1. Are there other organizations you see as competitors or peers?
2. What do you admire about how other mental health orgs operate?
3. Does the "music therapy" differentiation feel right? Is it central to your vision?

---

### 4. User Journey Review (15 min)

**Present the 5 personas:**

| Persona | Who They Are | Their Primary Need |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Marcus** | Man in crisis, 34 | Find support immediately |
| **Sarah** | Potential donor, 42 | Make meaningful impact |
| **David** | Volunteer, 38 | Host a group in his city |
| **Jennifer** | Sponsor, 45 | Corporate partnership |
| **Karen** | Family member, 52 | Help her son |

**Questions for each:**
1. Do these feel like real people you encounter?
2. Who is missing from this list?
3. Which persona is most important to serve well right now?
4. For crisis users: What happens today when someone contacts BBL in crisis?

---

### 5. Information Architecture Review (10 min)

**Present:**
- Proposed site map (26 pages total)
- Primary navigation: Home | Support Groups | Events | About | Shop | [Donate]
- Key pages: City-specific landing pages, Host application, Sponsor page

**Questions:**
1. Does this structure make sense for how you talk about BBL?
2. Are there pages that feel unnecessary?
3. Are there pages that are missing?
4. How important is the shop/merch? Should it be prominent or secondary?

---

### 6. Visual Direction Discussion (10 min)

**Present 3 mood board directions:**

| Direction | Feel | Best For |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **Midnight Brotherhood** | Dark, concert culture, therapeutic | Continuity with current brand, younger audience |
| **Grounded Growth** | Warm, earth tones, community | Broader appeal, healing feel |
| **Urban Canvas** | Bold, high-contrast, street culture | Strong statement, music-adjacent |

**Questions:**
1. Which direction feels most like BBL to you?
2. What visual references inspire you? (Other websites, brands, places)
3. Current site has dark theme with light blue accent - do you want to keep that or evolve?
4. How do you want men to feel when they land on the site?

---

### 7. Founder Story Discovery (20 min) â­ CRITICAL

**Set Up:**
> "The most powerful thing we can do is tell your story. Your 'why' is what will connect people to BBL. I'd like to ask you some questions - feel free to share as much or as little as you're comfortable with."

**Core Questions:**

**The Beginning:**
1. When did you start BBL? What year?
2. What was happening in your life at that time?
3. Was there a specific moment or person that made you say "I have to do something"?

**The Personal:**
4. Are you comfortable sharing your own mental health journey?
5. Have you lost someone to mental health struggles? (If yes, can we honor them?)
6. What did you wish you'd had during your hardest times?

**The First Steps:**
7. What was the very first thing you did to make BBL real?
8. Tell me about the first peer support meeting - who came? What happened?
9. Who was the first person to believe in this with you?

**The Challenges:**
10. What was the hardest moment in building BBL?
11. What did you get wrong at first?
12. How do you handle the emotional weight of this work?

**The Music:**
13. Why music? What role does it play in your life?
14. Why is it central to BBL's approach?

**The Name:**
15. How did you come up with "Bros Before Lows"?

**The Vision:**
16. Where do you see BBL in 5 years?
17. If you could reach every man struggling right now, what would you tell them?

**Capture:**
- Record this section (with permission)
- Take notes on quotable phrases
- Watch for emotional moments that reveal authentic story

---

### 8. Content & Gaps Discussion (5 min)

**Present content priorities:**
1. Founder story (video + written)
2. Testimonials from peer group members
3. "What is peer support?" explainer
4. Music therapy documentation
5. City-specific content

**Questions:**
1. Do you have photos from events/groups we can use?
2. Are there members who would share testimonials?
3. Can we film a peer support session (with consent)?
4. Is there media coverage we should reference?

---

### 9. Priorities & Timeline (5 min)

**Present priority framework:**

| Priority | Pages/Features |
|----------|----------------|
| **Must Have** | Homepage, Support Groups, City pages, Donate, Crisis resources |
| **Should Have** | About/Founder, Events, Host application |
| **Nice to Have** | Impact report, Sponsor tiers, Resource library |

**Questions:**
1. What's most urgent? (Fundraising? Group signups? Volunteer recruitment?)
2. Is there a timeline we're working toward? (Event, launch, grant deadline?)
3. What does success look like for this redesign?

---

### 10. Next Steps & Close (5 min)

**Summarize:**
- Key decisions made today
- Items that need follow-up
- What Mathieu needs to provide (photos, testimonials, story review)

**Confirm:**
- Timeline for design concepts
- Next meeting date
- Best way to communicate (email, text, calls)

**Close:**
> "Thank you for sharing so much today. Your story is the heart of this project, and I'm excited to bring it to life."
